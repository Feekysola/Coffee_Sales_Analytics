# ☕ Coffee Sales: Multi-Perspective Performance Analysis (Excel)

## 📄 Executive Summary
This project features a dual-layered data analysis workflow in **Microsoft Excel**, transforming raw transactional data into a suite of dynamic business intelligence tools. I developed two distinct analytical views: a high-level **Executive Sales Dashboard** for broad trends and a granular **Revenue Deep-Dive** focused on product-level performance. This project demonstrates advanced Excel capabilities in data modeling, interactive slicers, and strategic storytelling.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
The objective was to solve the "Operational Blind Spot" by answering two critical questions:
1.  **Macro View:** What are our global sales trends across different branches and months?
2.  **Micro View:** Which specific coffee types are driving revenue during different times of the day (Morning vs. Night)?

---

## 📽️ Analysis 1: Executive Sales Dashboard
This view focuses on high-level KPIs, monthly sales trends, and branch-level performance. It allows stakeholders to see the "Big Picture" of the business health.

[https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/16e4f52d-e638-4f3e-97fa-cb3bcd14d8c1]

**Key Insights:**
* **Monthly Velocity:** Identified specific peak months (e.g., March) where sales volume surged across all branches.
* **Top Branch Performance:** Successfully visualized revenue distribution across global locations, highlighting the most profitable company branches.
* **KPI Tracking:** Real-time calculation of Total Volume Sales and Highest Sales Coffee for any selected period.

---

## 📽️ Analysis 2: Revenue & Behavior Deep-Dive
This view utilizes interactive slicers to analyze customer behavior based on the **Day of the Week** and **Time of Day** (Morning, Afternoon, Night).

[https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a55881af-2966-494f-8918-350f122d3274]

**Key Insights:**
* **The 65% Revenue Drivers:** Confirmed that "Americano with Milk" and "Latte" are the primary revenue anchors, particularly during the morning and afternoon peaks.
* **Temporal Patterns:** Identified a distinct shift in product preference during "Night" sessions, where different coffee types see increased relative demand.
* **Slicer Interaction:** Demonstrated how the "Weekday" filters can be used to predict inventory needs for weekend vs. weekday rushes.

---

## 🛠️ Excel Technical Stack
* **Power Query:** For advanced ETL, merging tables, and data normalization.
* **Pivot Tables & Charts:** Creating dynamic summaries that respond to multiple slicers.
* **Advanced Slicers:** Implementing Timeline, Weekday, and Day-Time filters for a professional "App-like" user experience.
* **Data Modeling:** Establishing relationships between sales, products, and branches.

---

## 🚀 Business Recommendations
1. **Strategic Staffing:** Use the **Time of Day** analysis from the second video to optimize shift scheduling, ensuring more staff are present during the high-revenue "Morning" and "Afternoon" windows.
2. **Branch-Specific Promos:** Use the **Executive Dashboard** to identify lower-performing branches and launch localized marketing campaigns to boost their specific sales volume.
3. **Inventory Alignment:** Align bean and milk orders with the specific coffee types identified as "Revenue Drivers" in the deep-dive analysis.
